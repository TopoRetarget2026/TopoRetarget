TopoRetarget: Interaction-centric Retargeting for Dexterous Manipulation

Anonymous Submission

CoRL 2026 (Under Review)

Paper · Code (coming soon) · BibTeX

Abstract

Retargeting human hand-object interactions to dexterous robot hands is a key bottleneck for scalable manipulation learning. Existing methods either ignore contact topology or rely on heavy optimization that breaks down on novel embodiments and object shapes. We propose TopoRetarget, an interaction-centric retargeting framework that preserves contact topology via a lightweight Laplacian deformation. Combined with a simple RL tracking pipeline, our retargeted trajectories transfer zero-shot to a real Wuji Hand on cube reorientation and pen spinning, achieving 9.33 mm contact precision, 16.30° contact alignment, and +40.6 pp success on Pen-Twirl over prior art at <5 ms per frame.

Method

From an input human-object trajectory, a warm-up stage initializes the robot hand pose, then a topology-aware Laplacian optimization refines the fingertip contacts while preserving interaction structure.

Comparison

Compared to OmniRetarget, Mink, DexPilot and GeoRT, TopoRetarget avoids common failure modes such as missed contacts, penetration, and topology drift.

Real-world Results

Zero-shot sim-to-real rollouts on Wuji Hand: cube reorientation and pen spinning.

Generalization

Generalization across embodiments (MANO / Wuji / Leap) and object scales.
